The Process

What makes this different

Most analysis covers one company at a time. We read every major player in a sector simultaneously and synthesize what the group of companies reveals together.

1

Read Every Report

We go through the full annual report — MD&A, Chairman's letter, Directors' report, risk factors — for every major company in the sector. Not screener data. Primary sources.

2

Separate Signal from Noise

Every sector brief separates what all managements agree on (priced in by the market) from where they diverge (where the alpha lives). Consensus is context. Divergence is insight.

3

Ground Every Claim

No generic commentary, no "industry experts believe." Every statement traces to a specific company's disclosure. If we can't source it to an annual report, it doesn't make the brief.

4

Deliver the Cross-Read

The final section surfaces insights visible only when you read all companies together — the patterns, tensions, and strategic bets that individual analysis cannot reveal.

Anatomy of a Brief

Five sections. One investment edge.

Each sector brief follows a consistent framework designed for investors who want depth without noise.

Context

The Setup

Three lines of sector macro context. GDP, volumes, policy shifts — only what the companies themselves cite. Sets the stage without burying you in data.

Priced In

The Consensus

Themes where all managements agree. If every CEO says the same thing, the market already knows. This is what's in the price — useful for context, not for edge.

Alpha

The Divergence

Where companies disagree — on strategy, risk appetite, market positioning, capital allocation. This is the most valuable section. Divergence reveals where the investment alpha lives.

Accountability

The Credibility Check

What managements said last year vs. what actually happened. Track record matters. We hold annual reports accountable so you know whose forward guidance to trust.

The Edge

The Signal

5 non-obvious insights that emerge only from reading all companies in a sector together. These are cross-cutting themes invisible to single-company analysis — the reason this product exists. Patterns, tensions, and strategic fault lines that tell you where the sector is actually heading.
