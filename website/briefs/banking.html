Context

The Setup

Indian banking in FY2025: credit growth slowed to 10.9% (from 16.3%), RBI cut repo rate by 100 bps to 5.50%, and system-level gross NPAs hit a 14-year low of 2.4%. On the surface, a healthy sector in a rate-easing cycle. Beneath it — a deposit war, a governance crisis, and a widening gap between the best and worst performers.

Priced In

The Consensus

All six banks agree on these themes — which means the market already knows:

  1. India GDP at 6–6.5% in FY2026. Every single annual report opens with this. It's table stakes, not insight.
  2. Rate cuts will revive credit demand. 100 bps cut so far, accommodative stance. Banks expect lower EMIs to unlock retail borrowing. Consensus is unanimous — and therefore already in the stock price.
  3. Asset quality is at multi-year best. System GNPA at 2.4%. Every bank (except IndusInd) reported improving slippages. The "NPA cleanup is done" narrative is fully baked in.
  4. Digital is non-negotiable. HDFC processes 97% of transactions digitally, SBI's alternate channels at 98.2%, Axis owns 33.3% UPI payer market share. Every bank claims digital leadership. None has a decisive moat yet.
  5. Rural expansion is the next frontier. Branches, BCs, digital onboarding in Tier 3–6 towns. All five banks (ex-IndusInd) are racing into rural/semi-urban markets.

The Divergence

This is where it gets interesting. Read these annual reports side by side, and five fault lines emerge:

1. The CASA Crisis Isn't Equal

The gap between the best and worst ROA in a single peer set is 5.4x. The driver? Unsecured retail and microfinance — banks that went heavy into personal loans and MFI got punished. Banks that stayed selective (Kotak, ICICI) came out clean.

3. HDFC Bank Is Playing a Different Game

Post-merger HDFC Bank (Rs 39.1L Cr assets) is now larger than ICICI + Axis combined. But the numbers tell a restraint story:

  • Advances grew only 5.4% — deliberately throttled
  • Deposits grew 14.1% — absorbing HDFC Ltd's maturing borrowings
  • NIM at 3.48% — second-lowest among private peers
  • Mortgage book at Rs 8.36L Cr — largest in India, structurally low-NIM
The signal: ~15% of HDFC Ltd borrowings (Rs 43,000 Cr) mature each year in FY26/FY27. As these roll off and get replaced by deposits, NIM should expand. The question is whether the market is patient enough to wait.

4. IndusInd's Governance Blow-Up Is a Warning

IndusInd Bank's FY2025: derivative accounting discrepancy (2.35% of net worth), GNPA jumping from 1.92% to 3.13%, CEO resignation, RBI-appointed committee overseeing operations. PAT collapsed from Rs 8,950 Cr to Rs 2,643 Cr.

But here's the pattern: Kotak also faced an RBI embargo on digital onboarding in FY2025. Two of six banks in this peer set faced material regulatory action in the same year. That's not a one-off. The regulatory bar is rising, and governance risk is being re-priced.

5. SBI's Transformation Is Structural

SBI's ROA journey: -0.19% (FY2018) → 1.10% (FY2025). Operating profit crossed Rs 1 lakh crore for the first time.

The most underappreciated metric: domestic CD ratio at 69.71%. Every private bank is at 85–95%. SBI has 15–25 percentage points of headroom to grow credit without needing incremental deposits at the same rate. At the largest balance sheet in the country, that's an enormous lever.

The Edge

The Signal

5 things only visible when you read all 6 annual reports together:

1. The deposit war is the real story, not credit growth

Every bank talked about credit demand. But read the balance sheets — deposits are the constraint. HDFC Bank is deliberately suppressing credit growth to fix its deposit mix. Axis is leveraging Citi's premium deposit base. SBI's low CD ratio is a structural advantage no private bank can replicate. Whoever wins the deposit battle wins the cycle.

2. Unsecured retail was the great separator

Banks that went aggressive on personal loans and microfinance (IndusInd) got hit. Banks that stayed disciplined (ICICI's "fair to customer, fair to bank" philosophy, Kotak's selective underwriting) emerged stronger. This is a management quality signal, not a macro signal.

3. Gold loans are the stealth growth story

HDFC Bank grew gold loans 28%, SBI grew 53%. Three banks highlighted it as a strategic priority. The shift from unorganized gold lending to banks is early-innings and high-margin. This segment is flying under the radar.

4. The "merger premium" for HDFC Bank is being tested

The market priced in synergies at acquisition. But FY2025 shows the cost — lowest CASA, compressed NIM, subdued advance growth. The thesis requires patience through FY27. If HDFC Ltd borrowing runoff proceeds as planned, this is the best risk-reward setup in large-cap banking. If it doesn't, the derating has further to go.

5. PSU vs. private bank convergence is real

SBI at 19.87% ROE now exceeds ICICI (16.52%), Axis (16.52%), and Kotak (15.2%). The "PSU banks are structurally inferior" narrative needs updating. SBI is compounding at scale with a deposit franchise that private banks envy.
